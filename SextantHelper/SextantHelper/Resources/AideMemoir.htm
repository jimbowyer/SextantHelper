BASICS:

Latitude: Each degree of Latitude (N/S) ~69miles, crudely 1/60 degree (minute) is mile.

Longitude: longitude (W/E) differs between equator (69miles) and poles (zero as they meet).

1 hour of time = 15° latitude.

  • so 13:00 UTC at your local noon hour mean you are 15°
  • & 11:00 UTC at your local noon hour mean you are 15°E

1 minute of time = 0.25° latitude.

For longitude time results West is +ve, East is -ve

NOON SIGHT and EXAMPLE:

  • Latitude (N/S): use Sextant reading [e.g. 67° 44']
    • Take sextant readings close to noon and use best one/sun highest in sky
      • e.g. 89°60' - 67° 44' = 22° 16'
      • NOTE: 90° == 89° 60 :)
    • + Declination
      • look up for dec for date/hour in Almanac
      • Its added because we're in northern hemisphere, facing south, so +ve
      • 45° 40.7'N (North because +ve number in result)
      • If in doubt do minus too and see… should be obvious which hemisphere in! :)

  • Longitude (W/E) use watch time [e.g. 15:37 UTC]
    • UTC time - 12 hours (noon)
      • e.g. 15:37 - 12:00 = 03:37
    • Multiply by 15° e.g. 15° x 3hours 37min = 54° 15'W.
      • Break last step as follows:
      • 45° [from 3hours x 15° = 45°]

        • +

      • 9° 15' [from 37 min * 0.25° = 9.25° or (37/60 * 15 = 9.25) ….and… 0.25° x60' = 15'])
      • W because result is +ve number …and West is +ve, East is -ve.
